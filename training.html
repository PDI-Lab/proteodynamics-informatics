Hands-on Workshop

Unlocking Protein Dynamics

Join Our 5-Day Intensive Training on Computational Structural Biology

The Proteodynamics & Informatics Lab is excited to announce an upcoming intensive 5-Days Training on Computational Structural Biology: Hands-on Training and Advanced Analysis.

In modern structural biology, understanding how proteins move, fold, and interact is key to decoding complex pathologies—from neurodegenerative disorders to infectious diseases. This comprehensive training program is specifically designed to bridge the gap between biological theory and cutting-edge in silico execution, giving you the practical tools needed to drive impactful research.

📅 Curriculum

The 5-Day Curriculum at a Glance

Day 1

Introduction to In Silico Biology

  • Foundational concepts of computational and in silico structural biology.
  • Understanding the computational landscape and toolkits.

Day 2

Molecular Docking & Pharmacokinetics

  • Protein, ligand, and enzyme structural modeling and preparation.
  • Hands-on molecular docking workflows.
  • Evaluating the pharmacokinetic properties and profiling of potential drug ligands.

Days 3 & 4

Molecular Dynamics (MD) Simulation & Trajectory Analysis

  • Fundamental concepts of MD systems and rigorous simulation setup.
  • Executing production runs and implementing trajectory quality checks.
  • Advanced Trajectory Analysis: deep dive into data interpretation including RMSD, Rg, SASA, RMSF, eRMSF, PCA, DCCM, FEL, and DSSP.

Day 5

Project Design, Manuscript Writing & Submission

  • How to successfully identify critical research gaps from wet-lab papers.
  • Designing a bulletproof project workflow while avoiding common mistakes.
  • Crafting high-quality, publication-ready scientific figures.
  • Best practices for manuscript writing and navigating top-tier journal submissions.

🔬 Why Attend?

This program isn’t just about running software—it’s about mastering data interpretation. You will learn how to transform raw simulation trajectories into sophisticated, publication-grade analytical plots (such as Free Energy Landscapes, Dynamic Cross-Correlation Matrices, and Principal Component Analyses) that top-tier reviewers and elite institutions expect to see.

Training Curriculum — Detailed

Day-1

Introduction to Computational/In silico Biology.

Relevant concepts regarding Molecular docking and Pharmacokinetics Profiling of drugs. E.g., Protein, Ligand, Enzyme, Protein Structure Modeling and Preparation, Ligand Preparation, Molecular Docking, and Pharmacokinetics Properties of Ligands.

Instructors

Raju Dash, PhD

Postdoctoral Fellow · DGIST & KAIST

Dr. Raju Dash is an accomplished structural biologist and researcher with over 13 years of specialized experience in computational structural biology. This includes computational chemistry, computer-aided drug design, and protein structural dynamics. Dr. Dash holds a Ph.D. in Medicine with a major in Anatomy from Dongguk University, South Korea. Currently, he is serving as a postdoctoral fellow at two reputable institutes, the Department of New Biology at Daegu Gyeongbuk Institute of Science and Technology (DGIST) and InnoCORE AI-CRED Institute at Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST). He serves as an editor for several prestigious journals, including Scientific Reports, PLOS ONE, and IJMS.

Throughout his career, Dr. Dash has harnessed computational biology to investigate the mechanistic basis of protein function at the atomic level for proteinopathic neurodegenerative diseases and the clearance of misfolded protein aggregates. A prolific academic, his work is widely recognized within the scientific community, as reflected in a high Google Scholar citation count (over 4,000), an h-index of 33, over 110 publications in high-impact journals, and multiple Korean patents for the treatment of neurodegenerative brain diseases. His excellence in research was recognized with the “Best Graduate School Academic Award 2022,” encompassing the Research Grand Prize, Best Research Award, and Best Paper Award at Dongguk University.

Dr. Dash brings a strong foundation of teaching and hands-on training to his instructional roles. He has delivered special lectures on in silico molecular genetics for premedical courses at Dongguk University, Korea. To date, he has guided several research enthusiasts who are currently conducting groundbreaking research at prestigious universities across the USA and the EU.

Md. Abul Bashar, M. Pharm (Running)

Research Fellow · Islamic University, Kushtia

Md. Abul Bashar is an emerging computational structural biologist and researcher specializing in computer-aided drug design (CADD), molecular dynamics simulations, and structural bioinformatics. He holds a Bachelor of Pharmacy degree with high academic distinction from the Islamic University, Kushtia, Bangladesh. Mr Bashar is currently pursuing a Master’s in Pharmacy at the same institute.

As an instructor, Bashar bridges theory and hands-on implementation, backed by a diverse technical toolkit across different dry-lab methodologies. He possesses extensive practical experience with industry-standard computational packages, including Schrödinger Suite (Glide, QikProp, and Desmond), PyMOL, and Unix/Linux-based analysis workflows. His teaching capability covers critical aspects of computational structural biology, including molecular docking, ADMET (pharmacokinetics and toxicity) evaluation, and the interpretation of molecular dynamics (MD) simulations using modern analytical frameworks such as MDAnalysis and MDTraj. Bashar’s instructional insights are directly informed by his active contributions to cutting-edge scientific research.

Working in close collaboration with international researchers, including post-doctoral mentors like Dr. Dash, his work focuses on unraveling the pathophysiological mechanisms of neurodegenerative diseases—such as Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)—through structural dynamics and virtual screening. He is a published author with peer-reviewed research papers appearing in 5 high-impact international journals, including the International Journal of Molecular Sciences (IF: 4.9), Nutrients (IF: 5.0), and Current Pharmaceutical Design (IF: 2.6). His strong command over statistical analysis, data visualization tools and publication-grade figure design using students receive comprehensive guidance in translating raw computational data into high-quality scientific findings.
