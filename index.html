Proteodynamics & Informatics Research Group

Structural biology has revolutionized our understanding of the molecular processes that drive life. Yet, a fundamental gap remains in our understanding of how and why proteins operate through specific dynamics, motions, and excited states.

The primary objective of our research group is to decode the atomic basis of protein function - specifically, to map the structural motions that regulate the conformational transitions required for different biological functions and how a point (also known as missense) mutation affects these motions, and hence its conformational transition and a certain biological function, leading to a certain disease. In addition, our group is also interested in identifying novel compounds against different pathologies.

For this, our group harnesses in silico structural biological tools, including molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, etc., in combination with artificial intelligence (e.g., ML/DL). Additional tools we utilize include the Markov state model (MSM) for analyzing protein misfolding pathways.

Our Core Research Interests

1. Proteinopathies
2. Neurodegenerative Diseases
3. Infectious Diseases
4. Protein Clearance System
5. Natural Products
6. Protein Aggregation
7. Liquid-liquid Phase Separation

Instructors

Raju Dash, PhD

Postdoctoral Fellow | Computational Structural Biologist

Dr. Raju Dash is an accomplished structural biologist and researcher with over 13 years of specialized experience in computational structural biology. This includes computational chemistry, computer-aided drug design, and protein structural dynamics. Dr. Dash holds a Ph.D. in Medicine with a major in Anatomy from Dongguk University, South Korea. Currently, he is serving as a postdoctoral fellow at the Department of New Biology at Daegu Gyeongbuk Institute of Science and Technology (DGIST) and InnoCORE AI-CRED Institute at KAIST. He serves as an editor for several prestigious journals, including Scientific Reports, PLOS ONE, and IJMS.

His work is widely recognized with over 4,000 Google Scholar citations, an h-index of 33, over 110 publications in high-impact journals, and multiple Korean patents for treating neurodegenerative brain diseases. His excellence was recognized with the "Best Graduate School Academic Award 2022" at Dongguk University.

Md. Abul Bashar

M. Pharm (Running) | Computational Structural Biologist

Md. Abul Bashar is an emerging computational structural biologist and researcher specializing in computer-aided drug design (CADD), molecular dynamics simulations, and structural bioinformatics. He holds a Bachelor of Pharmacy degree with high academic distinction from Islamic University, Kushtia, Bangladesh, where he is currently pursuing his Master's.

As an instructor, Bashar bridges theory and hands-on implementation. He possesses extensive practical experience with industry-standard computational packages, including Schrödinger Suite, PyMOL, and Unix/Linux-based analysis workflows. Working in close collaboration with international researchers, his work focuses on unraveling the pathophysiological mechanisms of neurodegenerative diseases (like ALS). He is a published author with peer-reviewed papers in high-impact journals including IJMS, Nutrients, and Current Pharmaceutical Design.

Course Curriculum

Day-1

Introduction to Computational/In silico Biology.

Day-2

Relevant concepts regarding Molecular docking and Pharmacokinetics Profiling of drugs. E.g., Protein, Ligand, Enzyme, Protein Structure Modeling and Preparation, Ligand Preparation, Molecular Docking, and Pharmacokinetics Properties of Ligands.

Day-3 & 4

Fundamental concepts of molecular dynamics simulation, MD Simulation System setup, Production Run and Trajectory quality checking, Trajectory Analysis (RMSD, RG, SASA, RMSF, eRMSF, PCA, DCCM, FEL, DSSP, Interaction Analysis).

Day-5

Identification of Research Gap from wet-lab papers, Designing a project and common mistakes during designing, High-quality Figure preparation, Manuscript Writing, and Journal Submission.
